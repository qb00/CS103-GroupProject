About the Band


Green Day is an American Band that first hit the stage in 1987 with lead
vocalist & Guitarist Billie Joe Armstrong and bass player Mike Dirnt. A
few years later the band was joined by their first drummer Tre Cool. The
rock band took the rock world by storm in 1994 with their label debut album
Dookie. Selling over 10 millions copies in the US alone, the album shot the
band to immediate stardom. In attempts to chase their immediate
success, Green Day released Insomniac in 1995, Nimrod in 1997 and
Warning in 2000. While fans still enjoyed these albums, there was seemingly
something missing in comparison to their first studio album. It wasn't
until 2004 that the band recaptured that original boom with the release of
American Idiot.

Green Day began to dominate the 2000's and 2010's with even more releases
including 21st Century Breakdown and their album trio of ¡Uno!, ¡Dos!, ¡Tres!
Yet the media craze and performances didn't end with a couple of concerts here
and there. Green Day took on Broadway in 2010 with an adaptation of the popular
American Idiot. It seems a little strange, yes, but the band saw an opportunity to
do something a little outside the box for their genre. To no surprise, like they had
constantly done throughout the 2000s, it was a smash hit. Not only did fans come
in groves to see the musical, but critics as well. American idiot: The musical,
was nominated for three Tony Awards, the highest award you can receive for a play,
and won two for Best Lighting Design and Best Scenic Design.

You can still catch the band performing one of their 12 studio albums today. But if
concerts aren't your thing, you can always catch one of their 12 Broadway plays,
or even stay at home and watch through one of their 4 box sets or even hop on YouTube
to view one of their 56 music videos.
